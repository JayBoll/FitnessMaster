ChestWorkout PUSH-UPS

The push-up requires several muscle groups to work, unlike other exercises that require weights, it works the smaller muscle groups and uses them as stabilizers.

DECLINE PUSH-UPS

The decline push-up is a more difficult version of the basic push-up. The difficulty is increased by placing your feet on a higher level than your hands.

DIAMOND PUSH-UPS

Diamond push-ups are another challenging variation of push-ups performed by placing your hands close together until your thumbs and index fingers touch forming a “diamond” shape.

PUSH-UP HOLD

The push-up hold is an isometric (held) type of exercise that will improve your chest strength and definition and will also work your core, shoulders and arms.

WIDE PUSH-UPS

Wide push-ups make you use your pectoral muscles more to help you lift your body and this will improve the strength in you upper body.
